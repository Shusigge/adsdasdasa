Investigating & Reporting
on Major Events
I often engage in gathering information and writing about important events in society. Over the years, I may have the opportunity to investigate and provide reports on social, political, or economic issues.
Interviewing Witnesses or Experts
I might visit the scene of a traffic accident and interview witnesses to gather more information about the incident. Or, I might interview experts about social issues, such as environmental pollution.
Writing Articles for Newspapers or Websites
After collecting information, a reporter writes an article about an ongoing event. For example, I may write an article about a local election, analyzing the candidates and predicting the results.
Reporting Live from the Scene
I may need to report live from the scene during emergencies like natural disasters, major fires, or protests. I have to document the situation and relay information through media channels like television, radio, or social media.
Using Social Media to Update News
I use social media platforms (like Twitter, Facebook) to provide real-time updates during unfolding events. For example, I could post updates about a press conference or a special event in progress.
Taking Photos and Recording Videos
Many reporters today are responsible for not only writing but also taking photos or recording videos for their articles. For example, a reporter covering a protest might have to record video of the participants and capture the scenes at the location.
